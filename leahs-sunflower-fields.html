The Field

Grown from divs, gradients, and patience

Every bloom above is built from plain HTML elements and CSS — no images, no JavaScript, no SVG. Petals are rotated gradient shapes, seed heads are layered radial gradients, and the whole field sways on a single keyframe. The rows climb gradually from front to back, the way a real field rises toward the horizon.

The Season

A sunflower's summer, in four stages

From seed to harvest takes roughly 90 to 110 days. Here's how the field changes as the summer rolls on.

Sprout

Seedlings break the soil and unfold their first true leaves.

Days 1–14

Growth

Stalks shoot upward, sometimes growing several centimeters a day.

Days 15–50

Bud

Young heads form and track the sun from east to west each day.

Days 51–70

Bloom

Mature flowers face east and open fully — the field turns gold.

Days 71–100

Plan a Visit

The field is always open

No tickets, no hours — this field lives in your browser. A few ways to enjoy it:

Resize the window

The field stretches and reflows to fill any screen, from phone to widescreen.

Watch the sway

Each flower runs the same gentle 5-second sway, offset randomly so the field ripples like wind.

View the source

Open dev tools and pick a flower apart — every petal is an element you can inspect.
